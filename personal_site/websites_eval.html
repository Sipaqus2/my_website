Website Evaluations

Three Good websites:

  • skillshare.com

    I really like the use of large images and the limited amount of text that accompanies each one. Right off the bat the large darkened image of someone drawing with white text and a green sign-up button on top catches your attention and already gives you some sort of idea as to what the website is about. As you scroll down the home page the same green color of the button from before remains as an accent color which creates a sense of unity and is visually appealing against the darker images and background color, but also looks good with the white ones. The fonts they use are beautiful, they look professional, clean, and are bold enough where they need to be and they all look really nice together.

  • dickblick.com

    With this website I like the limited use of color as well, it's mostly black and white with red accents. I think it's nice how when the mouse hovers over a drop down menu in the top bar that it becomes red and that the categories in each one is in red and is a bigger size than the subcategories which are in grey until you hover over them and then they turn red. This website also has a set of images that change between each other in a loop with a small box of text over them. I like how the categories of art supplies have images and how nice and organized all of the images for the products are. This website also has a set of fonts that are clean and sharp and add a level of professionalism. I really appreciate that this website gives you three different options to display the products: list view, detail view, and grid view.

  • hollar.com

    Hollar also sticks to a pretty simple limited color palette consisting of mostly white, black, and a bit of bright orange. The fact that the prices of products are in orange makes them stand out which is good since theyâ€™re trying to get you to buy stuff. On here the categories and dropdown menu also change color when hovered over which is nice. The name of the website and the shopping cart as well as the clearance and $1 shop are in the same orange color which looks nice and once again draws your attention to them. I really like the font they used for the name of the website and the other fonts are clean and simple, the font used for item descriptions are nice and thin.


    • Three Bad websites:

  • yahoo.com

    This website is very cluttered looking and has a ton of ads. There's so much to look at that you don't really know where they want you to look exactly, and as you continue to go down the page you find even more clutter with even more ads, some of which are those annoying video ads. Using it as a search engine it doesn't seem so bad or annoying and is actually useable, but when it comes to news, regardless of the specific kind, you'll find that the article is surrouned by ads and other extra junk which is unpleasant to look at and would discourage me from reading it.

  • wetcanvas.com

    I think that most of the text, especially the text for the categories, are way too small. It's really simple and looks pretty basic and plain. I don't really like the look of the website or the way that it's set up where there's a bunch of different forums within each of the categories. It means that to look at many different artworks I'd have to browse through several forums. It's kind of inconvenient.

  • starvinartist.com

    The design has way too much white space and the font and colors for the texts make it look very childish. It does not look professional. They also sell a very small amount of art supplies and all these things considered, I'm not sure that I'd trust this site enough to actually buy anything from it.
